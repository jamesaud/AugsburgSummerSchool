Location:

The class will spend their first week and a half in Augsburg, joined by a group of American and Italian students.

Then a 7 hour bus ride is taken through the Alps of Switzerland to the Italian city of Bergamo. The students will spend another week and half here. Bergamo is located just thirty minutes from Milan by train and is a beautiful city positioned on a mountain. The students will be staying in the lower part of the city in a hostel, and the coursework will be happening at the University of Bergamo in the upper city. Students can commute by walking or public transit.

Professors:

The faculty teaching this class include esteemed professors David Audretsch, Erik Lehmann, and Silvio Vismara.

David Audretsch is a Distinguished Professor at Indiana University. His research relates to entrereneurship and government policy with an emphasis on Europe and The United States.

Erik Lehmann is a professor at Augsburg University, known for his research in the field of Business Administration with a focus on Business Management and Organization. His specialization is on Corporate Governence and Strategy and Structure.

Silvio Vismara is a Professor at the University of Bergamo. He has made several significant contributions in the field of Business and Finance, with many papers specifically relating to IPO's in Europe.

Course Description

The students will be expected to work in international groups in order to complete a term paper on a selected topic by the end of the three weeks. In addition, each team will have to present their papers as a lecture in front of their classmates and professors. Because there is an emphasis on completeing the paper and project, there will be many free days throughout the class where students will be expected to work in their groups on the projects.

However, these free days are also an opportunity for the students to travel to different locations in Germany and Italy. Balancing the project time with your free time is an essential part of succeeding in this class.

In the real world your teams won't always be with your native language speakers -- and such is the case with this class. Teams will be multicultural, with the Germans, Americans, and Italians united into teams. Students will be free to pick their teams for the most part, as long as it is a multinational group.

The papers and presentations for this class will be in English. and the topics chosen for the projects will likely be focused on European countries and politics. Part of the class is understanding the material, but equally important is for the students to have the experience of working in diverse teams with people from different countries than their own.
